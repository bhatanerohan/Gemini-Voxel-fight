# PvP Gameplay Notes

## Core Direction

The game should feel fun in PvP because players are not only fighting aim and movement, they are fighting each other's weapon ideas. The forged-weapon system is the main differentiator and should drive the match flow.

## What Makes It Feel New

- Players carry 4 weapon slots with distinct roles instead of 4 damage variants.
- Matches are short and adaptation-heavy, with loadouts changing between rounds.
- Counterplay should be readable: players need to understand why they lost and what tool could answer it next round.
- Weapons should create mind games, space control, and combo windows, not just raw damage output.

## Recommended Slot Roles

- Primary: reliable baseline weapon.
- Control: trap, freeze, pull, wall, zone denial.
- Mobility or escape: dash, blink, reposition, movement burst.
- Finisher: high-risk, high-reward burst tool.

## Match Structure

Best-fit PvP loop:

1. Start a short arena round.
2. Players fight with 4-slot forged loadouts.
3. Round ends quickly.
4. Each player gets a short forge or swap window for 1 slot.
5. Next round starts with updated counters and mind games.

This is stronger than pure deathmatch because the meta evolves during the match.

## Mechanics That Support PvP Depth

- Status setup into payoff combos.
- Defensive counters such as shields, reflects, barriers, or cleanses.
- Information tools such as reveal, decoys, cooldown disruption, or smoke/distortion.
- Arena elements such as jump pads, moving cover, hazards, or destructible voxel chunks.

## Design Rule

Avoid making every weapon independently useful in the same way. If every forged weapon is just a different damage pattern, PvP becomes noisy and shallow. Weapons should change timing, positioning, and opponent decisions.

## Best First Implementation Order

1. Build round-based PvP first, not open-ended deathmatch.
2. Enforce weapon category roles across the 4 slots.
3. Add between-round single-slot swap or forge flow.
4. Add a few hard counters such as shield, blink, trap, and reveal.
5. Add clear death summaries or replay hints so players can understand what happened.
